Subsurface scattering psychovisual experiment

Hii, I'm Robin! For the course Perception in Visual Computing I'm doing a study on how we perceive translucency.

Objects like orange slices, cheese, and human skin are slightly permeable to light. Through a process called subsurface scattering, these objects "glow" under the correct conditions. Can you see how the front face of the cheese "glows" slightly because it allows light to pass through?

I'm evaluating various rendering methods to produce images of objects that give the illusion of this property.

So, what do you have to do?

There is a reference image on the left, and a test image on the right. The slider under the two images adjusts the material type of the test object. The material lets more light pass through it when the slider is moved to the right and less when it is move to the left.

It is your task to match the material type of the test image to the material type of the reference image as close as possible.

Note that illumination conditions of the test and reference differ in some cases. Your task is to match the material type, this means that the two objects with the same material may have different overall brightness. This means that if you feel that some reference object does not let any light pass through it, then move the corresponding slider to the fully left position. Even if this means that the overall brightness of the two images is very different.

When you matched all the images, click submit.
